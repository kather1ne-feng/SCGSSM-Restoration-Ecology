Updates

Our Math Team was able to calculate the area of our irregularly shaped plot by using Geospatial Pin Analysis, or satellite GPS measurement modeling, establishing a blueprint for which baseline data may be easily collected for future use in our research. The large plot has successfully been segmented into smaller triangles (that we have also measured) so that we can more readily tally, monitor, and manage plant growth in the future. The plots have also been categorized as A, B, or C, and given a number (for example: A13, B2, C3). We are currently counting the species in each plot to create an inventory of all the species present in our site and measure its current biodiversity.
